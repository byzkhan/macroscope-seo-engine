# Macroscope Content Style Guide

## Voice and Tone
- **Confident and direct** — no hedging ("might", "perhaps", "it's possible that")
- **Second person** — address the reader as "you" / "your team"
- **Active voice** — "AI review catches bugs" not "bugs are caught by AI review"
- **Present tense** — "Macroscope integrates with GitHub" not "will integrate"

## Structure
- **H1**: One per article. Front-load the keyword. Under 60 characters.
- **H2**: Major sections. 4-7 per article. Should be scannable as a standalone outline.
- **H3**: Subsections within H2s only. Use sparingly.
- **No H4-H6** unless absolutely necessary for FAQ sub-answers.
- **Short paragraphs**: 2-4 sentences max. One idea per paragraph.
- **Bullet lists**: For 3+ parallel items. Use bullets, not numbers, unless order matters.
- **Code examples**: Fenced markdown blocks with language tag. Keep under 15 lines.

## Content Standards
- **Word count**: 1,500-2,500 words for standard posts. 2,500-4,000 for pillar content.
- **Direct answer**: Core question must be answered within the first 2 paragraphs.
- **FAQ section**: Required. Minimum 4 questions. Answers should be 40-60 words.
- **Internal links**: Minimum 3 per article to macroscope.com paths.
- **External links**: To authoritative sources only (research papers, official docs).
- **Code examples**: At least 1 per technical article. Real-world, not contrived.

## SEO Conventions
- **Title**: Under 60 chars. Keyword front-loaded. No clickbait.
- **Meta description**: 120-155 chars. Includes primary keyword. Compelling but accurate.
- **Slug**: Lowercase, hyphenated, keyword-rich. Max 5-6 words.
- **Keyword density**: 0.5-2.5% for primary keyword. Natural placement.
- **Heading keywords**: Primary keyword in H1 and at least 2 H2s.

## Words and Phrases
### Use
- "catches", "detects", "identifies", "flags" (for AI review findings)
- "integrates with", "works with" (for tool compatibility)
- "engineering teams", "developers" (for audience)
- "pull requests", "PRs" (not "merge requests" unless GitLab-specific)
- "code review", "review process" (not "code inspection")

### Avoid
- "Revolutionary", "game-changing", "cutting-edge", "state-of-the-art"
- "Best in class", "industry-leading", "world-class"
- "Simply", "just", "easily" (dismissive of complexity)
- "Obviously", "clearly" (condescending)
- "Leverage" (use "use"), "utilize" (use "use"), "implement" (use "add" or "set up")
